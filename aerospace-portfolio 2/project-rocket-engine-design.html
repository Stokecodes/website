Rocket Engine Design

High-performance liquid fuel rocket engine with optimized combustion chamber design

Project Overview

This project focused on designing and analyzing a high-performance liquid fuel rocket engine optimized for maximum thrust-to-weight ratio and combustion efficiency. The design incorporates advanced cooling techniques and optimized nozzle geometry for superior performance.

Through extensive CFD analysis and thermodynamic modeling, the engine design achieved 98.5% combustion efficiency with a specific impulse of 450 seconds, representing a significant improvement over conventional designs.

Technical Approach

Combustion Chamber Design

Optimized chamber geometry using CFD analysis to ensure complete fuel mixing and minimize pressure losses while maintaining stable combustion.

Cooling System

Implemented regenerative cooling system using fuel as coolant, reducing engine weight while improving thermal management and fuel efficiency.

Nozzle Optimization

Designed bell-shaped nozzle with optimized expansion ratio for maximum thrust generation and minimal flow separation at various altitudes.
